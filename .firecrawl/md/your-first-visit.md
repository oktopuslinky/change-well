# Your First Visit

## **What to expect?**

#### **If in person or through telehealth, your first therapy session has two main goals:**

###### **1\. To Assess Your Circumstances**

We will use the first meeting to understand your current circumstances and the reasons you are seeking therapy. We will explore whether our areas of expertise align with your needs.

We will begin by identifying which type of therapy may be most helpful and discussing what the process may look like based on your goals. This may include practice techniques, guided reflections, or small actions to complete between sessions. We will also talk through what progress can look like and how we can work together to support it. Your active participation is an important part of the healing process.

###### **2\. To Build A Relationship**

A strong therapist-client relationship is central to our approach, so your first session will feel like a two-way conversation. We will take time to get to know you, while also helping you get a sense of who we are. During this time, we will discuss your primary concerns, your history, and the experiences that matter most.

You can expect:

- Compassion, empathy, and respect
- A therapist who listens carefully and values your perspective
- Evidence-based techniques and information
- A safe and confidential environment
- Practical strategies you can apply in daily life
- A personalized plan for treatment

### Confidentiality

![](https://www.change-well.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/1128/2020/02/5e3e430d3349b.jpg)

Therapy sessions are confidential, and what you share will remain private unless you provide written consent.

There are a few legal exceptions where a therapist is required to share information to ensure safety. This may occur if:

- There is suspected abuse of a child, dependent adult, or elderly person
- A client presents a serious threat of harm to another person
- A client is at risk of harming themselves, and safety cannot be maintained within therapy

In these situations, therapists are legally required to take appropriate steps to protect those involved.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**What happens during the first therapy session?** _Your first session focuses on understanding your current concerns, background, and goals for therapy. Your therapist will ask questions to better understand your situation and determine the best approach moving forward._

**How long are therapy sessions?** _Most therapy sessions are approximately 50 minutes. Your therapist may recommend a session frequency based on your needs and treatment goals._

**Do you offer virtual sessions?** _Yes. Change-Well offers both in-person sessions in Plano and secure telehealth sessions for clients who prefer meeting remotely._

**Do you accept insurance?** _Change-Well accepts many insurance plans. The office can help verify your coverage and explain any out-of-pocket costs before your first appointment._

**Do you offer self-pay options?**

_Yes. We offer self-pay options for clients who prefer not to use insurance or do not have coverage. Our team can provide clear pricing information and help you determine the best option for your care._

**What should I bring to my first appointment?** _Before your first visit, you will complete intake paperwork and any required forms. Bringing any relevant medical or psychological history can also help your therapist better understand your needs._

**How many therapy sessions will I need?** _The number of sessions varies for each individual. Some clients attend therapy for a short period to address a specific concern, while others continue therapy for longer to support ongoing growth and progress._
